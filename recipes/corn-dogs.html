Corn Dogs

Description

I love the corn dogs you buy at carnivals but wanted to make them at home so I created this easy batter for frankfurters. Great served with mustard.

A corn dog is a sausage (usually a hot dog) that has been skewered, coated in a cornmeal batter, and deep-fried. A staple of American cuisine, the street food was likely invented by German immigrants in Texas in the 1920s.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 quart vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 (16 ounce) packages beef frankfurters
  • wooden skewers

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Combine cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl; stir in milk and egg to make a batter
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  4. Meanwhile, pat frankfurters dry and insert a skewer into each one.
  5. Roll frankfurters in batter until well coated.
  6. Fry 2 or 3 corn dogs at a time in preheated oil until lightly browned, about 3 minutes.
  7. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
